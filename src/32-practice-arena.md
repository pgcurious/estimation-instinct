# 32 — The Practice Arena

*Reading built recognition. This builds recall — one randomized rep at a time, out loud, until the numbers arrive before you ask for them.*

Every other chapter in this book is something you *read*. This one is something you *do*. Below is an infinite generator of estimation problems: a random system at a random scale, every time you press the button. You estimate each number; it reveals the worked five-rung solution using the same cheat-sheet canon the whole book runs on, and grades you where it matters — by **order of magnitude**.

This is the practice engine the book was written around. The 30-day program tells you *when* to practise; the Anki deck drills the *anchors*; this drills the *move* — the full climb from users to money, under a little mild pressure, on a system you have never seen.

## How to use it

The discipline is three steps, and the middle one is the whole point:

1. **Read** the prompt and the givens.
2. **Say each answer out loud** — the number *and* the one-line chain that produced it — *before* you type anything. "Ten thousand a second, because a billion a day over ten-to-the-five." Speaking is the rep. Typing is just scoring.
3. **Reveal**, and read the worked solution even when you were right — especially when you were right, to check that your *chain* matched, not just your number.

Grading is order-of-magnitude on purpose, because that is how interviews grade: **within ~3× is spot on**, within 10× is close, beyond 10× is a real miss. Precision beyond one significant figure is theatre.

Do **five a day, interleaved** — never the same system twice in a row. The streak counter rewards clean full-problem runs; it resets the moment a single number lands off by more than 10×. Chase the streak. (Your score persists in this browser only — it is between you and the arena.)

> ⚡ **Instinct check** — Before you press the button the first time: a system has 100M DAU doing 10 actions each a day. Average QPS? Say it before you read on. *(10^9 ÷ 10^5 = 10^4 — ten thousand a second. If that arrived instantly, you are ready. If it didn't, that is exactly what this page is for.)*

## When you outgrow it

The arena trains the spine — the mechanical climb. Real rounds add two things it can't: an interviewer who pushes back, and a system whose *shape* you have to infer. For the first, run the **pushback rounds** in the Part III and V walkthroughs aloud. For the second, take any product you use today, and climb the ladder on it cold before you've read anyone's answer. When the arena's problems feel slow rather than hard, you have the instinct. Then the job is only to keep it — which is the next chapter.

## Numbers to keep

- The rep is **speaking**, not typing — say the number and its chain before you reveal.
- **Within ~3× is a win.** One significant figure. Precision beyond that is theatre.
- **Five a day, interleaved** beats twenty on a Sunday — the schedule is in the next chapter.
- A reset streak is data: it names the exact rung your instinct still guesses instead of knowing.
